RESEARCH INTERESTS

My PhD research compares local responses to homelessness in Vancouver, Calgary, Toronto and Montréal. Broader research and teaching interests include Canadian politics, social policy and urban governance.

In the absence of a comprehensive federal and in most cases provincial response to homelessness, the local level is the source of significant policy leadership in responses to homelessness. But homelessness is governed very differently in each of these four cities; I ask what explains the difference in responses to homelessness, and consider what consequences this has for social protection in Canada. Through over 70 semi-structured interviews with actors direclty involved in ending homelessness in Canada, I find that an important factor that shapes the governance network of homelessness is the problem defition of homelessness at the local level.
